What This Means For You:

While the bank advertises a 7.5% interest rate on your fixed deposit, your actual wealth growth after accounting for inflation and taxes is only 1.68% per year.

Inflation erodes the purchasing power of your money over time. What costs ₹100 today will cost ₹105.20 in a year at 5.2% inflation.

In real terms, a ₹500,000 deposit will only grow by ₹8,400 in purchasing power over 1 year despite the bank paying you ₹38,720 in interest.
Compounding Effect: Your FD compounds quarterly, which increases your returns slightly, but still doesn't compensate for the combination of inflation and taxes.
